Title: Protecting Marine Life--Let Our Actions Set Sail

The oceans are precious gems of the Earth, hosting a diverse range of life. However, the marine ecosystem is in great peril due to human activities that are improper and often disregarded.

Additionally, marine mammals fall prey to marine pollution. Animals like dolphins, whales, and sea lions, which are rare, often ingest plastic debris in the ocean, posing a significant risk to their survival. The accumulation of pollutants can adversely affect their immune, nervous, and reproductive systems. The survival of marine life and the balance of the entire ecosystem are under threat due to marine pollution.

Marine birds are also affected by this pollution. They unintentionally consume planktonic plastics, leading to choking, digestive issues, or damage to internal organs. These wonderful creatures become casualties of our irresponsible actions. We cannot remain passive and watch them struggle helplessly in polluted oceans. Sea turtles are also endangered by ocean pollution. Sea turtles inadvertently consume plastic bags, confusing them for food, resulting in choking, gastrointestinal issues, and eventual fatality. These turtles are crucial to the marine ecosystem, and their extinction would cause irreparable harm.
